'Lego Masters' Gears Up For Season 4 Premiere, Announces Release Date

Publish date: 2024-07-11

TALENTRECAP.COM More Shows LEGO Masters

CR: Tom Griscom/FOX

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Lego Masters season four is fast approaching, and fans are definitely excited to witness another thrilling series. The show is coming back this September, promising bigger builds and heightened drama.

Lego Masters to Premiere on September 28

Since July, the Fox reality competition series has been teasing the fourth season. According to its trailer, viewers must get ready to “unlock your imagination in a brand new way.”

“Teams of two LEGO enthusiasts go head-to-head, with infinite possibilities and an unlimited supply of LEGO bricks. Throughout the competition, host Will Arnett and expert judges encourage the amateur builders, introduce incredible challenges and put the creations to the test,” Fox explained.

Essentially, the competing pairs who will managed to impress the judges will advanced to the next round. In the finale, top teams will face off for the ultimate cash prize and Lego trophy.

The show has not yet revealed who the contestants will be in the upcoming season. Nonetheless, Will Arnett will return as host, joined by expert judges Jamie Berard and Amy Corbett who will help him in finding the Lego Master.

Lego Masters season four premieres Thursday, September 28, at 9 pm ET/PT.

Fans Can’t Wait for the Upcoming Season to Begin

In its most recent post, Lego Masters teased the show once more by showing a trio pic of Arnett, Berard, and Corbett. In its caption, the series expressed that the three are already excited for season four’s kickoff. Consequently, fans took the time to engage with the post and reveal their excitement for the upcoming premiere.

“Super pumped for the new season!” one fan wrote.

“So glad they wrapped filming before this strike. I don’t know what I would have done without a new season,” another person commented.

Meanwhile, one fan expressed their wish for season four to have concentrate the show the game, not the drama.

“Hopefully there’s less ‘reality’ nonsense and more focus on the amazing builders and their creations,” they wrote.

Lego Masters will be sharing Thursday nights Gordon Ramsay’s Hell’s Kitchen season 22.
