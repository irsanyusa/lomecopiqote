The 33-year-old wowed the judges with her final bake, a showstopper named "Ode to the honey bee," which was a multi-layered Entremet containing blackberry jelly, honey, lemon curd, and lavender mousse.

You might assume that Faldo dedicates her life to baking — but she happens to also be a competitive track cyclist and a former army officer training to be a stuntwoman.

This is 33-year-old Surrey-born Sophie Faldo, who just won this year's "Great British Bake Off."

She lives in Surrey with her boyfriend David, who is rumoured to have entered her into the competition.

Controversy has surrounded her living situation, however. On her application, she claimed to be living with David, but reports revealed that she is claiming a single person's benefit allowance, allowing her more than if she was living as a couple.

According to The Sun, a "Bake Off" spokesman said: "Sophie has recently declared a change in her circumstances to the DWP. A meeting to reassess her situation was postponed by her local job centre until next week. Any suggestion of benefit fraud relating to Sophie is ­categorically untrue."
