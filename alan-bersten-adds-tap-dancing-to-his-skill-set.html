Alan Bersten Adds Tap Dancing to His Skill Set

Publish date: 2024-07-18

TALENTRECAP.COM Talent Dancing With The Stars

Getty Images

Reading Time: 2 minutes

Dancing with the Stars professional dancer Alan Bersten recently surprised his fans with a video of him tap dancing on Instagram. Alongside the video, the ballroom professional admitted that he’s excited to learn a new form of dance. His enthusiasm and eagerness were evident as he embarked on a brand new dance journey, featuring renowned tap dancer Chloe Arnold.

Alan Bersten Learns To Tap Dance

Bersten’s Instagram post started off with expressing his gratitude to Chloe Arnold, the highly accomplished tap dancer, choreographer, and co-founder of the tap dance company Syncopated Ladies. As he opened the package containing his tap dancing shoes, Bersten’s anticipation was very clear.

The video then transitioned to a slow montage of Bersten’s tap dance lessons with Arnold. With her wealth of knowledge and expertise in tap dancing, Arnold guided Bersten through various techniques and steps.

Bersten’s caption reflected the sheer delight he experienced during his tap dance lessons with the pro. His words conveyed the genuine joy and fulfillment he felt throughout the process. In his caption, he invited Chloe’s sister, Maud Arnold, to join them in the future. Within the post, he hinted at the possibility of a tap dance collaboration between the trio.

His gratitude towards Arnold, his tap dance mentor, was evident as he embarked on this exciting learning experience. The video captured the joy, determination, and excitement that Bersten brought to his tap dance lessons. His video definitely has inspired his fans and fellow dancers to try something new.

Chloe Arnold Praises Alan Bersten’s Ability To Learn

In the comment section of Bersten’s post, he and Arnold revealed their mutual admiration following the class. The professional tap dancer made sure to share that Bersten is a quick learner.

“Talk about a quick study!!! Omg!! You’re so fun and fast!!! Geez! Day 1” Arnold wrote.

Grateful for Arnold’s guidance, Bersten responded with appreciation, acknowledging her as an exceptional teacher. He thanked her and eagerly anticipated their next collaboration, writing, “You’re the best teacher!! Next time you’re doing ballroom!”

As Bersten and Arnold continue to inspire and delight audiences with their dancing talents, their interaction in the comments section served as a testament to the joy and camaraderie that dance can certainly foster. It also showed the power of social media in bringing dancers and fans closer together, allowing for shared moments of celebration and anticipation.
